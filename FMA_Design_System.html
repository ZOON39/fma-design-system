Usage rules

Use black on white for headlines and primary CTAs — maximum contrast.
Use #F2F2F2 as the page/section background — never as a text colour.
Use #333 for body copy — softer than pure black at smaller sizes.
Two distinct blues are confirmed: Prime tee uses deep navy #141975; Pulse shorts use mid steel blue #4A6B97. Never interchange them — they belong to different products.
Seasonal tones (sage, sand) — one at a time, never combined with blue or pink in the same post.
Never use neon, bright hues, or high-saturation colours. Keep all accents muted and calm.

Visual style — observed patterns

Bold condensed-weight type fills the frame width on static posts — text is the hero, not a caption.
White logo lockup always bottom-left; website URL bottom-right on all static posts.
Coastal and outdoor photography — warm natural tones, golden light, Perth-evoking locations.
Dark overlays on photography posts ensure white logo/text remains legible.
Dusty pink and blue product shots use clean white or neutral studio backgrounds.
Reels are lifestyle-first — movement, mindset messaging, or ambient coastal scenes. Never salesy.

Brand strategy

Positioning

We are: A Western Australian lifestyle activewear brand built around mental wellbeing through movement. Calm, premium, coastal.
We are not: Gymshark. We don't sell performance metrics, body transformation, or gym culture. No hustle language. No before/after content.
Price positioning: $60–$120 AUD premium. Value is in quality and brand identity — never compete on price.
Launch phase: Perth-led. Then national Australia. Then international expansion.

Target audience

Primary — women 25–45

Active, health-conscious, lifestyle-led. Yoga, jogging, coastal walks, pilates. Values wellbeing, mental health, time outdoors. Follows brands that match her values — not just her wardrobe.

Secondary — men (Prime range)

Same lifestyle values, different cut. Not a separate audience segment — men who share the movement-as-wellbeing philosophy. No separate messaging required at launch.

Pre-launch strategy (now → 11 May 2026)

Dual CTA approach: Awareness posts (Breathe + Live + Move pillars) build brand recognition. Sign-up posts drive the email list with LAUNCH20 as the incentive.
Launch discount: Code LAUNCH20 — 20% off. One-time acquisition tool. Does not set a discount-led precedent.
Email marketing: Not yet configured. Priority post-launch — build Klaviyo welcome flow + abandoned cart sequence.
Influencer plan: Perth-based micro influencers aligned with coastal/lifestyle/wellness values. Activate around or after launch date.

Shopify product copy approach

Structure: Short punchy headline → then practical detail (fabric, fit, care).
Example headline: "Designed to move with you — not against you."
Then: Fabric composition, sizing guidance, care instructions — factual, concise, confident.
Never: Transformation language, body-aesthetic claims, or superlatives like "the best legging you'll ever own."
